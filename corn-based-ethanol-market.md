<p><strong>The Corn-Based Ethanol Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Wet Milling Process Ethanol</li><li>Dry Milling Process Ethanol</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The corn-based ethanol market primarily consists of two process types: wet milling and dry milling. In wet milling, corn is soaked and processed to separate its components, producing ethanol along with valuable by-products like corn oil and gluten. Dry milling, on the other hand, involves grinding the entire kernel, fermenting the starches directly into ethanol, and generating distillers grains for animal feed. Both methods cater to different market needs, with wet milling offering diversified products and dry milling focusing on efficiency and lower production costs.</p></p>
