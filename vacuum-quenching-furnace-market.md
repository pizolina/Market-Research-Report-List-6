<p><ul><li>GasQuenching</li><li>LiquidQuenching</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The vacuum quenching furnace market is segmented into gas quenching and liquid quenching. Gas quenching utilizes inert gases, such as nitrogen or argon, to cool materials rapidly, offering uniform heat treatment with minimal oxidation. In contrast, liquid quenching employs various fluids, like oil or water, to provide faster cooling rates, enabling effective hardening of metals. Both methods enhance material properties, with gas quenching being suitable for complex geometries and liquid quenching favored for more straightforward applications requiring aggressive cooling.</p></p>
