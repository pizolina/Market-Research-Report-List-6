<p><p>The low alcohol beer market encompasses various production methods, primarily limit fermentation and dealcoholization. Limit fermentation involves controlling yeast activity to restrict alcohol production while retaining desired flavors. In contrast, the dealcoholization method removes alcohol from fully brewed beer, typically through technologies like evaporation or membrane filtration. Both methods cater to the growing consumer demand for healthier and lower-alcohol options, appealing to those seeking flavorful beverages with reduced alcohol content, making them popular choices in the beverage industry.</p></p>
