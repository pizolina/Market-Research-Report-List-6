<p><p>The X-ray Photoelectron Spectroscopy (XPS) market is divided into two main types: monochromatic and non-monochromatic XPS systems. Monochromatic XPS utilizes a single wavelength of X-rays, which enhances energy resolution and provides more precise chemical state information. In contrast, non-monochromatic XPS employs a broad range of X-ray energies, offering higher intensity but lower resolution. Each type caters to different analytical needs, with monochromatic systems preferred for detailed analysis and non-monochromatic systems used for quicker, less precise examinations.</p></p>
