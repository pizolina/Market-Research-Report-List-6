<p><p>The canned soup market comprises two primary types: condensed and ready-to-eat (RTE). Condensed soup requires water or broth to be added before consumption, appealing to consumers who prefer customization. This type is often favored for its longer shelf life and versatility in recipes. On the other hand, RTE soups are fully cooked and require no preparation, catering to busy lifestyles and convenience-seeking consumers. Both types offer diverse flavors, aiding in the meeting of varying consumer tastes and preferences.</p></p>
