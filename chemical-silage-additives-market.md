<p><p>The chemical silage additives market consists primarily of organic and inorganic acids, each serving distinct roles in silage preservation. Organic acids, such as lactic and acetic acid, promote fermentation by lowering pH, enhancing nutrient retention, and improving animal digestibility. In contrast, inorganic acids, including formic and propionic acid, help inhibit spoilage organisms and reduce microbial activity, thus extending shelf life. Together, these additives optimize fermentation processes and enhance the quality and safety of silage for livestock feed.</p></p>
