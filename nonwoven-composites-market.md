<p><p>The nonwoven composites market comprises several key types: meltblown, spunbonded, staples, and others. Meltblown nonwovens are produced by extruding molten polymer, resulting in fine fibers ideal for filtration and medical applications. Spunbonded nonwovens, created by spinning continuous filaments, offer strength and durability, commonly used in hygiene products and geotextiles. Staple nonwovens utilize short fiber fibers, known for their versatility in hygiene, automotive, and insulation applications. "Others" incorporate innovative techniques and materials, expanding the market's scope.</p></p>
