<p><p>Complex Phase (CP) steel is categorized into cold-rolled and hot-rolled types, each serving distinct applications. Cold-rolled CP steel is produced at lower temperatures, resulting in superior surface finish and dimensional accuracy, making it ideal for automotive and structural applications. In contrast, hot-rolled CP steel is processed at higher temperatures, offering improved formability and toughness. This type is commonly used in construction and heavy machinery, where strength and durability are paramount. Both types contribute significantly to the evolving steel market.</p></p>
