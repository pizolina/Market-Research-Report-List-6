<p><p>Microwave diodes refer to electronic devices that are used in microwave circuits to rectify, detect, switch, and modulate microwaves. The main types of microwave diodes include Schottky diodes, Gunn diodes, Tunnel diodes, Zener diodes, and PIN diodes. Schottky diodes are commonly used in high-frequency applications due to their low forward voltage drop. Gunn diodes generate microwave signals through the Gunn effect. Tunnel diodes exhibit negative differential resistance. Zener diodes are used for voltage regulation. PIN diodes provide fast switching speeds and high breakdown voltage in microwave circuits.</p></p>
