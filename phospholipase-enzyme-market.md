<p><p>The phospholipase enzyme market is categorized into four main types: Phospholipase A, which hydrolyzes fatty acids, commonly used in food and pharmaceutical industries; Phospholipase B, which acts on both the sn-1 and sn-2 positions of phospholipids, facilitating cell membrane modulation; Phospholipase C, which generates inositol phosphates and diacylglycerol for cell signaling, and Phospholipase D, involved in the production of phosphatidic acid, critical for membrane dynamics. Each type serves diverse applications across biotechnology, food processing, and pharmaceuticals, driving market growth.</p></p>
