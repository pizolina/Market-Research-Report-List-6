<p><p>The Transcervical Female Sterilization market includes two main types: Tubal Ligation and Transcervical Implants. Tubal Ligation involves surgically blocking or sealing the fallopian tubes to prevent pregnancy. This procedure is more invasive and requires hospitalization. In contrast, Transcervical Implants, such as the Essure device, use a non-surgical approach where small coils are placed in the fallopian tubes through the cervix, leading to scarring and blockage over time. Both methods offer effective, long-term contraception solutions for women.</p></p>
