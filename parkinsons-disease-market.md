<p><p>Parkinson's Disease treatment market includes various medication types aimed at alleviating symptoms. Carbidopa/Levodopa is the most common, enhancing dopamine levels to improve motor function. Dopamine receptor agonists mimic dopamine in the brain, providing an alternative to traditional treatments and often used in early-stage disease. MAO-inhibitors prevent the breakdown of dopamine, extending its availability in the brain. Together, these medication categories address the diverse needs of patients and contribute to an evolving therapeutic landscape for Parkinson's management.</p></p>
